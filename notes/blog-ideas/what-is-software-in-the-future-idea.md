# SaaS Workflow Framework: Bespoke vs Productized × Flexible vs Guided

## Axes
- **X-axis:** Bespoke → Productized  
- **Y-axis:** Flexible → Guided  

---

## Quadrant Framework

|                     | **Flexible** | **Guided** |
|---------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Bespoke** | **DIY Systems** | **Adaptive Systems** |
| **Productized** | **Tool Platforms** | **Workflow Software** |

---

## Quadrant Definitions & Examples

### 1. Bespoke + Flexible → DIY Systems

**Definition:**  
User-created, highly flexible tools with little built-in structure. Workflows are defined manually each time.

**Characteristics:**
- Maximum flexibility
- No enforced process
- High user effort and variability
- Difficult to scale or standardize

**Examples:**
- Excel spreadsheets for financial modeling or inventory tracking
- Notion docs used as lightweight CRMs or project trackers
- Custom GPTs used ad hoc for task support
- Internal scripts or one-off Python notebooks

---

### 2. Productized + Flexible → Tool Platforms

**Definition:**  
General-purpose software that provides building blocks but leaves workflow design to the user.

**Characteristics:**
- Configurable but not prescriptive
- Broad applicability across industries
- Requires user-defined processes
- Acts as a “toolkit”

**Examples:**
- Airtable (custom workflows built on top of tables)
- Salesforce (when used as a flexible CRM rather than enforced pipeline)
- QuickBooks journal entries (manual accounting workflows)
- BI tools like Looker or Tableau

---

### 3. Productized + Guided → Workflow Software

**Definition:**  
Opinionated, structured software designed for a specific workflow or industry use case.

**Characteristics:**
- Predefined workflows
- Strong guidance and automation
- Built for repeatability and scale
- Core of traditional SaaS

**Examples:**
- Shopify checkout and order management flows
- Gingr (pet services vertical SaaS)
- Inventory systems that automatically generate accounting entries
- Industry-specific ERPs (manufacturing, healthcare, etc.)

---

### 4. Bespoke + Guided → Adaptive Systems

**Definition:**  
Highly tailored systems that enforce structured workflows, often generated or adapted dynamically (increasingly AI-driven).

**Characteristics:**
- Custom to a specific user or organization
- Strong workflow enforcement
- Dynamically generated or adapted
- Combines customization with structure

**Examples:**
- AI-generated ERP systems tailored to a company’s operations
- Custom GPT/agent that manages workflows end-to-end (e.g., inventory → accounting → reporting)
- Internal AI agents enforcing approval flows or operational processes
- Dynamically generated tools based on company data and behavior

---

## Key Insight

Historically:
- **It costs time and money to move from productized to bespoke and/or from productized to guided**
- **Only high paying customers got bespoke guided products**

Now:
- **Adaptive Systems (Bespoke + Guided)** are becoming cheaper with AI
- This quadrant represents the **next evolution of SaaS**.

Future:
- What is the limit with agentic AI? Will AI be able to make everything as bespoke and guided for free (intuitive custom agents for all!)? Or will there still be some need for a guidance layer provided by a SaaS builder?
- My prediction is that many users will want someone to build their adaptive system for them.
