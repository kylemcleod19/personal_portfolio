## The Problem With Most PRDs

Most product specs are written for the author, not the reader. They're long, they bury the decision, and they leave engineers guessing about what "done" looks like.

Here's a format that works better.

## The One-Page Spec

```
Problem: [one sentence — what user pain are we solving?]
Hypothesis: [if we build X, we expect Y, as measured by Z]
Out of scope: [explicit list of what we are NOT doing]
Success criteria: [specific, measurable, time-bound]
Open questions: [decisions still needed + owner + deadline]
```

That's it. Everything else — wireframes, edge cases, technical notes — goes in appendices that engineers can pull when they need them.

## The Key Principle

**Lead with the problem, not the solution.** Engineers are problem-solvers. Give them the problem first and they'll often suggest solutions better than the one you had in mind.

## What To Skip

- Background sections longer than 2 sentences
- Stakeholder alignment details (put those in a separate doc)
- Exhaustive edge case tables before the core flow is agreed on

Start lean. Expand only when the team asks for more.
