## The Problem

New users were abandoning our product within the first session at an alarming rate. Activation — defined as completing a core action — was hovering at 23%.

## Research

We ran 18 user interviews and analysed session recordings across 500 new sign-ups. Three themes emerged:

- Users didn't understand what the product did within the first 30 seconds
- The setup wizard asked for too much information upfront before showing any value
- Error states during onboarding had no recovery path

## What We Changed

### 1. Value-first framing
We rewrote the first screen to show a live demo of the core action before asking users to sign up. Conversion from landing page to sign-up went up 11%.

### 2. Progressive disclosure
We collapsed the 8-step wizard into 3 required steps, deferring optional settings to a "complete your profile" nudge shown on Day 3.

### 3. Guided empty states
Every empty state now includes one recommended action with a sample dataset users can clone in one click.

## Results

> "I actually got value out of this on the first day — that's rare." — User interview, post-launch

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Activation rate | 23% | 41% | +78% |
| Time to first action | 14 min | 8 min | −40% |
| Day-7 retention | 31% | 38% | +22% |

## Lessons Learned

Ship the smallest version of the improvement first. Our first hypothesis was to rebuild the entire wizard — we would have spent 6 weeks building something that wasn't the core problem.
