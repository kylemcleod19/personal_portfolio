## Step 3 — Persona Reviews

**Edge case — mostly empty site:** If the site is mostly placeholder/empty (fewer than 2 real content pieces across all areas), skip persona reviews entirely. Instead, output a sequenced build-from-scratch list of `/content-advisor` calls and stop.

**Edge case — single voice requested via `$ARGUMENTS`:** Run only that voice, skip cross-voice synthesis, go straight to Step 6 handoff.

For each active persona, produce exactly this structure:

```
### [Persona] Review
**Lens:** [what they're looking for]
**Strong signals:** [2-3 things that work]
**Gaps / concerns:** [3-5 items, each tagged: 🔴 high / 🟡 medium / 🟢 low]
**Verdict:** [one-sentence overall impression]
```

### Voice 1 — VC

**Mindset:** "Would I take a meeting?" Pattern-matching, impatient, blunt.

**Evaluates:**
- Differentiated thesis (not generic "AI is changing everything")
- Evidence of building, not just thinking — repos, live products, shipped artifacts
- Portfolio depth — more than one project
- Operator vs. commentator signal — did this person do the work or just observe it?
- Memorability — is there a hook that sticks after closing the tab?

### Voice 2 — Hirer

**Mindset:** "Would I move to phone screen?" Structured, checklist-oriented.

**If a JD is available** (from reference JDs or `$ARGUMENTS`): Map each key requirement to site evidence using:
- ✅ Evidenced
- ⚠️ Partially evidenced
- ❌ Not addressed

**If the JD is ambiguous**, state the interpretation of the role before reviewing.

**Default lens** (no JD): Director/VP Product at AI-forward Series A-C startup.

**Evaluates:**
- Career progression and scope growth
- Quantified outcomes (revenue, users, speed, efficiency)
- Leadership evidence — team size, cross-functional coordination, hiring

- Functional contact page — can they actually reach this person?

### Voice 3 — Referral

**Mindset:** "Would I forward this link?" Casual but discerning. This is a peer who might text the link to a founder friend.

**Evaluates:**
- 10-second clarity — does the site make sense immediately?
- Something memorable to reference when sharing ("check out the person who…")
- Professional polish — no broken links, placeholder text, or amateur styling
- Working CTA — clear way to get in touch
- Nothing embarrassing visible — placeholder content, broken images, lorem ipsum

- List gaps that only one voice raised, with that voice's severity rating

---

## Step 5 — Clarifying Questions

Before generating handoffs, ask 2-3 clarifying questions about:
- Priorities — which gaps matter most to the user right now?
- Constraints — timeline, content they have vs. need to create, sections they want to defer
- Direction — anything they disagree with or want to push back on

**Wait for the user to answer before proceeding to Step 6.**

---

## Step 6 — Content-Advisor Handoff

Generate 3-5 ranked `/content-advisor` invocations. Each must be specific enough that content-advisor skips its "what do you want to work on?" intake step.

Format each as a ready-to-run command:

> **Priority 1:** `/content-advisor Rewrite the About section on the home page to include team leadership evidence — the current version only shows solo work. Add 1-2 sentences about leading cross-functional teams and quantified outcomes.`

> **Priority 2:** `/content-advisor Add a second work entry in src/content/work/ for [project name]. Focus on: the problem, what the user personally did, measurable outcome. Use the catch-ai.mdx frontmatter schema as template.`

Each handoff should:
- Name the specific file or section to change
- State what's wrong or missing
- Describe what the new content should accomplish
- Be detailed enough to act on without further clarification
